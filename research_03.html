Climate Sensitivity

Overview: We explore how climate sensitivity varies with different patterns of radiative forcing using a coupled ocean-atmosphere model. It finds that Southern Ocean forcing doubles climate sensitivity compared to tropical forcing. The tropics show reduced cloud feedback due to strong surface-to-troposphere coupling, while the extratropics exhibit strong positive cloud feedback with weaker coupling. These findings challenge the conventional view that tropical surface temperature changes regulate climate sensitivity and have implications for understanding asymmetric forcings like geoengineering and volcanic eruptions.
