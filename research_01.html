Precipitation Diurnal Cycle

Global climate models often struggle to accurately simulate daily convection cycles, particularly over land where peak precipitation occurs too early. A new convective closure approach enhances interaction between shallow and deep convection, improving diurnal patterns significantly in atmosphere-only and ocean-atmosphere coupled models. This adjustment delays peak land precipitation by about three hours and reduces diurnal phase biases by half compared to satellite observations.
